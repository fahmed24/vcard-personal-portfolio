About me

I’m a Cloud and DevOps Engineer based in Southern California, focused on building reliable, scalable infrastructure. I care about how systems operate in the real world—how services are deployed, pipelines perform, and stability is maintained at scale.

I’ve worked across consulting and enterprise environments: automating cloud systems at Ingram Micro, where I designed workflows that cut Dev/QA operating costs by 50%; managing cloud infrastructure and identity at McKinsey; and leading multi-tier application migrations at Slalom. My core work centers on Terraform, AWS, Kubernetes, and the CI/CD systems that tie everything together.

Beyond client work, I maintain a personal infrastructure platform. A production-grade Proxmox cluster running Kubernetes, fully managed with Terraform and Ansible. It leverages GitOps via ArgoCD, centralized identity through Keycloak, secrets management with Vault, and a complete observability stack (Prometheus, Grafana, Loki, Tempo). An NVIDIA GPU powers real workloads, including local LLM inference and hardware-accelerated media processing. I use it to design, test, and stress systems, validating patterns before they reach production environments.

This platform also serves as a **live demo environment**—a secure, production-like system you can log into and explore, with SSO and read-only access to real services.
